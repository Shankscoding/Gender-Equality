Power of Equality

Iceland

Iceland leads in gender equality, was the first to ban unequal pay, and has strong female political participation. It also pioneered by electing the world’s first female president in 1980.

Sweden

Sweden has a low maternal mortality rate (4 per 100,000 births) and a high women’s labor force participation (61.7%). It was the first country to introduce gender-neutral parental leave in 1974.

Norway

Norway became the first country to mandate that at least 40% of corporate board members must be women, increasing female leadership in business. Norway’s gender equality policies, including equal pay and parental leave have boosted economic growth and female workforce participation.

Finland

Finland is a stable democracy with free healthcare, education, and strong social security. The government actively works to close the gender pay gap through transparency and equal pay policies. Finland is one of the safest countries for women, with strong laws against discrimination and domestic violence.

New Zealand

New Zealand ranks high in happiness, with strong democracy, low corruption, free healthcare, and excellent work-life balance. It embraces multiculturalism, promotes an active outdoor lifestyle, and ensures safety. First to Grant Women’s Voting Rights – In 1893, New Zealand became the first country to grant women the right to vote.
